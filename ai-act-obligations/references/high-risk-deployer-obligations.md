# High-Risk AI System — Deployer Obligations (Art. 26)

## Legal Basis

Art. 26 AI Act — Obligations of deployers (Betreiber) of high-risk AI systems.

*German: Pflichten der Betreiber von Hochrisiko-KI-Systemen*

**Recitals:** 91-96

---

## Complete Obligation Set

### A. Technical Operational Measures (Technische Maßnahmen)

#### A1. Use Per Operating Instructions — Art. 26(1)

**Obligation:** Use the high-risk AI system in accordance with the instructions for use accompanying the system.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 26(1) |
| Frequency | Continuous |
| Time effort | 2/5 |
| Expertise required | 2/5 |
| AI competency | 2/5 |
| RACI | IT=R, Legal=A, Business=I |

**What this means:**
- Read and follow the provider's instructions for use
- Do not use the system outside its intended purpose
- Operate within documented parameters and conditions
- Ensure deployment environment matches provider specifications

---

#### A2. Monitor System Operation — Art. 26(5)

**Obligation:** Monitor the operation of the high-risk AI system on the basis of the instructions for use and, where relevant, inform providers in accordance with Art. 72.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 26(5) |
| Frequency | Regular/continuous |
| Time effort | 3/5 |
| Expertise required | 3/5 |
| AI competency | 3/5 |
| RACI | IT=A, Technical=R, Legal=C |

**What this means:**
- Actively monitor system performance during operation
- Track output quality, accuracy, and anomalies
- Document monitoring activities and findings
- Report issues to provider per Art. 72

---

#### A3. Ensure Input Data Relevance — Art. 26(4)

**Obligation:** Ensure that input data is relevant and sufficiently representative in view of the intended purpose of the high-risk AI system, to the extent the deployer exercises control over the input data.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 26(4) |
| Frequency | Regular |
| Time effort | 3/5 |
| Expertise required | 3/5 |
| AI competency | 3/5 |
| RACI | IT=A, Business=R, Legal=C |

**What this means:**
- Verify quality and relevance of data fed into the system
- Check for data completeness, accuracy, and representativeness
- Ensure no prohibited data categories are included
- Document input data governance procedures

---

#### A4. Retain Auto-Generated Logs — Art. 26(6)

**Obligation:** Keep the logs automatically generated by that high-risk AI system to the extent such logs are under their control, for a period appropriate to the intended purpose of the high-risk AI system, of at least six months, unless provided otherwise in applicable Union or national law.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 26(6) |
| Frequency | Continuous |
| Time effort | 2/5 |
| Expertise required | 2/5 |
| AI competency | 2/5 |
| RACI | IT=R, Legal=A, Compliance=C |

**CRITICAL: 6-month minimum retention period. Must be in place from first day of deployment.**

**What this means:**
- Configure log storage with minimum 6-month retention
- Ensure logs are not tampered with or deleted
- Make logs available to authorities upon request
- Consider longer retention if national law requires it

---

### B. Organizational Measures (Organisatorische Maßnahmen)

#### B1. Assign Qualified Oversight Persons — Art. 26(2)

**Obligation:** Assign human oversight to natural persons who have the necessary competence, training and authority, as well as the necessary support.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 26(2) |
| Frequency | One-time setup + ongoing |
| Time effort | 3/5 |
| Expertise required | 4/5 |
| AI competency | 4/5 |
| RACI | HR=R, Legal=A, IT=C |

**What this means:**
- Formally designate person(s) responsible for human oversight
- Ensure they have: (a) necessary competence, (b) training on the specific system, (c) authority to override/intervene, (d) organizational support
- Document assignment and qualifications
- Provide ongoing support per Art. 26(2) second sentence

---

#### B2. Inform Affected Persons — Art. 26(11)

**Obligation:** Inform natural persons who are subject to decisions taken or materially influenced by the high-risk AI system that they are subject to the use of the high-risk AI system.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 26(11) |
| Frequency | Before/at time of use |
| Time effort | 3/5 |
| Expertise required | 3/5 |
| AI competency | 2/5 |
| RACI | Legal=R, Communications=A, Business=C |

**What this means:**
- Proactively inform persons affected by AI system decisions
- Information must be provided before or at the time the system is used
- Must be clear, concise, and accessible
- Overlaps with GDPR Art. 13/14 transparency obligations

**GDPR cross-reference:** Consider preparing a combined AI Act/GDPR transparency notice.

---

#### B3. Inform Employees/Works Council — Art. 26(7)

**Obligation:** Before putting into service or using a high-risk AI system at the workplace, inform workers' representatives and the affected workers that they will be subject to the use of the high-risk AI system.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 26(7) |
| Frequency | Before deployment |
| Time effort | 3/5 |
| Expertise required | 3/5 |
| AI competency | 2/5 |
| RACI | HR=R, Legal=A, Works Council=I |

**What this means:**
- Notify workers' representatives (Betriebsrat in Germany) before deployment
- Inform individual affected workers
- Provide information about how the AI system will be used
- Consider works council co-determination rights under national labor law

**GDPR cross-reference:** Consider preparing employee AI transparency documentation.

---

#### B4. AI Competence Training — Art. 4

**Obligation:** Ensure that staff and other persons dealing with the operation and use of AI systems on their behalf have a sufficient level of AI literacy, taking into account their technical knowledge, experience, education and training and the context the AI systems are to be used in.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 4 |
| Frequency | Initial + periodic updates |
| Time effort | 4/5 |
| Expertise required | 4/5 |
| AI competency | 4/5 |
| RACI | HR=R, Management=A, Legal=C |

**Note:** Art. 4 applies to ALL operators of ALL AI systems (not just high-risk). For high-risk systems, the competence requirements are higher.

---

#### B5. Incident Reporting — Art. 26(5) Third Sentence

**Obligation:** Where the deployer has reasons to consider that the use of the high-risk AI system may present a risk, it shall inform, without undue delay, the provider or distributor and the relevant market surveillance authority, and shall suspend the use of that system.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 26(5) s.3 |
| Frequency | Event-driven |
| Time effort | 5/5 (when triggered) |
| Expertise required | 4/5 |
| AI competency | 3/5 |
| RACI | Legal=R, IT=C, Management=A |

**What this means:**
- Establish incident detection and reporting procedure
- When risk identified: (1) inform provider/distributor, (2) inform market surveillance authority, (3) suspend system use
- "Without undue delay" — similar urgency to GDPR breach notification
- Document all incidents and responses

**GDPR cross-reference:** If personal data is involved, consider establishing a parallel GDPR breach notification procedure (Art. 33/34).

---

#### B6. Register Use in EU Database — Art. 49(3)

**Obligation:** Before putting into service or using a high-risk AI system listed in Annex III, deployers that are public authorities, EU institutions/bodies/agencies, or deployers acting on their behalf shall register themselves and select the system, and register its use, in the EU database.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 49(3) |
| Frequency | One-time |
| Time effort | 2/5 |
| Expertise required | 2/5 |
| AI competency | 1/5 |
| RACI | Legal=R, IT=C |

**Note:** Registration obligation for deployers primarily applies to public sector deployers. Private deployers may also need to register depending on national implementation. For a step-by-step registration guide, see [references/eu-database-registration.md](references/eu-database-registration.md).

---

#### B7. Cooperate with Authorities — Art. 26(12)

**Obligation:** Cooperate with the relevant national competent authorities in any action those authorities take in relation to the high-risk AI system.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 26(12) |
| Frequency | Event-driven |
| Time effort | 3/5 (when triggered) |
| Expertise required | 3/5 |
| AI competency | 2/5 |
| RACI | Legal=R, Management=A, IT=C |

### C. Impact Assessments

#### C1. Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) — Art. 26(9)

**Obligation:** Use the information provided under Art. 13 to carry out a data protection impact assessment under Art. 35 GDPR where applicable, before putting the high-risk AI system into service.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 26(9) + Art. 35 GDPR |
| Frequency | Before deployment (review periodically) |
| Time effort | 5/5 |
| Expertise required | 5/5 |
| AI competency | 3/5 |
| RACI | Legal/DPO=R, IT=C, Management=A |

**GDPR cross-reference:** Perform a DPIA per Art. 35 GDPR, incorporating the provider's Art. 13 information.

---

#### C2. Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA) — Art. 27

**Obligation:** Before deploying a high-risk AI system referred to in Art. 6(2) [Annex III], deployers that are bodies governed by public law, or are private entities providing public services, and deployers using systems under Nr. 5(b) and 5(c) of Annex III, shall perform an assessment of the impact on fundamental rights.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Legal basis | Art. 27 |
| Frequency | Before deployment |
| Time effort | 5/5 |
| Expertise required | 5/5 |
| AI competency | 3/5 |
| RACI | Legal=R, Compliance=A, HR/Operations=C |

**Who must perform FRIA:**
- Public authorities and bodies
- Private entities providing public services
- Deployers of insurance risk assessment systems (Annex III Nr. 5(b))
- Deployers of social benefits eligibility systems (Annex III Nr. 5(c))

**FRIA content (Art. 27(1)):**
- Description of deployer's processes using the AI system
- Period and frequency of use
- Categories of natural persons and groups affected
- Specific risks of harm to those groups
- Human oversight measures
- Measures to address identified risks
- Date and applicable period of the assessment

For a detailed FRIA methodology, proportionality framework, worked example, and fillable template, see [references/fria-template.md](references/fria-template.md).
