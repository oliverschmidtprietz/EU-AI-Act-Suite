# AI Omnibus 2026 — Timeline postponements

## Original Art. 113 timeline (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 as adopted June 2024)

| Provision | Original effective date |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Art. 6(2) (Annex III high-risk obligations) | 2 August 2026 |
| Art. 6(1) (Annex I high-risk obligations) | 2 August 2027 |
| Art. 111(2) legacy cut-off (systems placed on market before date X) | 2 August 2026 |

## Postponed dates under the AI Omnibus 2026

The AI Omnibus (legislative package proposed by the Commission and progressing through co-legislators) postpones these dates:

| Provision | Original date | **Omnibus date** |
|-----------|---------------|------------------|
| Art. 6(2) (Annex III) | 2 August 2026 | **2 December 2027** |
| Art. 6(1) (Annex I) | 2 August 2027 | **2 August 2028** |
| Art. 111(2) legacy cut-off | 2 August 2026 | **2 December 2027** |

These dates are now confirmed in the Commission's Draft Guidelines on Art. 6 classification (general principles, ¶448).

## Implications

### For providers and deployers

- Annex III high-risk obligations apply ~16 months later than originally planned.
- The legacy cut-off extension means more systems already in service before the new date benefit from the Art. 111(2) "no significant change" carve-out.
- Public-authority deployers must still comply by 2 August 2030 regardless (Art. 111(2) second sentence).
- Large-scale IT systems (Annex X) must comply by 31 December 2030 (Art. 111(1)).

### For this skill

- All effective-date references in [SKILL.md](../SKILL.md) and downstream artefacts use the Omnibus dates.
- The Commission guidelines themselves are dated 2026 (post-Omnibus); they cite the postponed dates.
