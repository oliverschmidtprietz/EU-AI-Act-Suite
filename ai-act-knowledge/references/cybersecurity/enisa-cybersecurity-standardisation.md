## Overview

Publication date: March 14, 2023.

The overall objective of this document is to provide an overview of standards (existing, being drafted, under consideration and planned) related to the cybersecurity of artificial intelligence (AI), assess their coverage and identify gaps in standardisation.

The report achieves this by:

- Considering the specificities of AI, and in particular machine learning.
- Adopting a broad view of cybersecurity, encompassing both the "traditional" confidentiality-integrity-availability paradigm and the broader concept of AI trustworthiness.
- Examining how standardisation can support the implementation of the cybersecurity aspects embedded in the proposed EU regulation laying down harmonised rules on artificial intelligence (the AI Act).

## Context: Standards Mapping for AI Cybersecurity

The report maps AI-specific cybersecurity concerns to existing and developing standards frameworks. It addresses the gap between traditional cybersecurity standards (focused on the confidentiality-integrity-availability triad) and the broader requirements of AI trustworthiness, which include robustness, transparency, fairness, and accountability.

### Relationship to the AI Act

The report examines how standardisation can serve as an operational tool for regulatory compliance with the AI Act's cybersecurity requirements, particularly:

- The cybersecurity requirement under Article 15 for high-risk AI systems.
- The role of harmonised standards in providing a presumption of conformity.
- Gaps where existing standards do not adequately address AI-specific cybersecurity challenges (e.g., adversarial robustness, data poisoning, model extraction).

### Standards Landscape

Key standardisation bodies and activities relevant to AI cybersecurity include:

- **ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42** — Artificial intelligence (international standards for AI).
- **CEN/CENELEC JTC 21** — Artificial Intelligence (European standardisation).
- **ETSI** — Standards for secure AI and related telecommunications aspects.
- **ISO/IEC 27000 series** — Information security management, extended for AI contexts.
- **IEC 62443** — Industrial cybersecurity, relevant to AI in operational technology.

### AI-Specific Cybersecurity Challenges for Standardisation

The report identifies areas where AI-specific threats require new or adapted standards:

- **Adversarial attacks** — Manipulating inputs to cause incorrect AI outputs.
- **Data poisoning** — Compromising training data to alter model behaviour.
- **Model extraction and inversion** — Stealing or reverse-engineering AI models.
- **Supply chain risks** — Vulnerabilities in pre-trained models, datasets, and AI toolchains.
- **Confidentiality of training data** — Preventing inference attacks that extract private data from models.
- **Robustness and resilience** — Ensuring AI systems maintain correct behaviour under adversarial conditions.

### Gaps Identified

The report highlights several areas where standardisation gaps exist:

- Lack of comprehensive standards for AI-specific threat modelling.
- Limited coverage of adversarial robustness testing methodologies.
- Insufficient standards for lifecycle security of AI systems (from training through deployment and monitoring).
- Need for harmonised approaches to AI cybersecurity certification.
- Gaps in standards addressing the intersection of data protection and AI security.
