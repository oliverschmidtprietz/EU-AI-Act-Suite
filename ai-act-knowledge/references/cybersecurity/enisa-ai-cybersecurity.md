## The Changing AI Landscape

- AI is now a core part of many products and services.
- AI is transforming cybersecurity — new opportunities but also new threats and challenges.
- Cybersecurity threats: Advanced GPAI can, for example, exploit vulnerabilities, create adaptive malware, and automate cyber-attacks or phishing.
- The EU is responding: The EU AI Act is the world's first comprehensive AI regulation to promote uptake and innovation in trustworthy AI.

## The AI Act as a Strategic Asset

Key characteristics of the AI Act:

- **Technology neutral** — horizontal product safety legislation
- **Risk-based approach** — applying rules commensurate to risk
- Classic EU internal market rules with coherence with EU acquis on product safety
- Focus on AI systems — no regulation of technology, but of use cases
- Covers risks to health, safety and fundamental rights

## AI Act — A Unified Approach to Safety and Security

The EU AI Act leads a paradigm shift:

- AI cybersecurity is one of the essential requirements of AI system safety.
- Cybersecure-by-design is not optional — it is legally mandated.
- Organisations must treat cybersecurity as an integral feature of AI system development, not a separate domain.
- Cybersecurity is not just security of IT infrastructure.

## Risk-Based Rules for AI Systems

- **AI with unacceptable risk is prohibited** — e.g., social scoring, certain biometrics
- **Requirements for high-risk AI** — e.g., AI in employment, medical devices
- **Disclosure for AI with transparency risks** — e.g., chatbots that impersonate humans
- **No rules for AI with minimal risk** — e.g., AI-enabled videogames, spam filters
- **Transparency and risk management** for general-purpose AI models that can be components of AI systems

## Requirements and Standards for High-Risk AI Systems

Mandatory requirements for high-risk AI systems before they can be used on the EU market:

1. Risk Management
2. Data Governance and Data Quality
3. Record Keeping and Logging
4. Transparency
5. Human Oversight
6. Accuracy
7. Robustness
8. Cybersecurity
9. Quality Management
10. Conformity Assessment

The provider is responsible for the EU declaration of conformity and CE marking.

### Harmonised Standards

- Operational tools to support regulatory compliance with the requirements under the AI Act.
- Ongoing work in ISO/IEC SC-42 and CEN/CENELEC JTC-21.
- Main principle: "international first" — build on IEC/ISO work as much as possible, as long as international standards are aligned with the AI Act objectives and approach and cover the same types of risks.

## General-Purpose AI Models

General-purpose AI models are highly capable AI models used as the basis of AI systems (such as ChatGPT).

### Disclosure Obligations and Risk Management for GPAI Models

- **Transparency** for all general-purpose AI models.
- **Risk management** for those with systemic risk.
- Code of practice developed together with stakeholders will detail out the rules.

## AI Act Interplay with the Cyber Resilience Act

- CRA sets horizontal cybersecurity rules for all digital products.
- AIA sets AI-specific cybersecurity rules.
- Both AIA and CRA are part of the EU "New Legislative Framework" safety acquis.
- Standards will play a key role.
- Presumption of conformity + one conformity assessment procedure for AI-enabled products (default rule: the AI Act).

## Recap: The Changing Paradigm

Cybersecurity is a core part of the AI Act product safety approach. The Act explicitly includes cybersecurity as one of the essential requirements of high-risk AI system safety. This means: **a high-risk AI system that is not cybersecure is, by definition, not safe under the EU AI Act.**

### Requirements for High-Risk AI Systems

- Implement technical measures to manage and mitigate risks including cybersecurity risks (RMS and QMS).
- Prevent data manipulation and data vulnerabilities.
- Maintain logging and comply with transparency and human oversight.
- Ensure resilience against adversarial attacks.
- Comply with state-of-the-art cybersecurity practice.

Standards serve as compliance tools.

## The AI Act's Governance Structure

- **Commission (AI Office)** — rules for general-purpose AI models.
- **Market surveillance authorities** — rules for AI systems (prohibitions, high-risk, transparency).
- **AI Board** with EU Member States to coordinate at EU level.
- **Scientific Panel** supports with independent technical advice.
- **Advisory Forum** supports with stakeholder input.

### The European AI Office

The AI Office has a 360-degree vision on AI:

- Key role in the implementation of the AI Act, especially in relation to general-purpose AI models.
- Fosters research and innovation in trustworthy AI.
- Positions the EU as a leader in international discussions and contributor to AI for good.

Units include: AI Regulation and Compliance, AI Safety, Excellence in AI and Robotics, AI for Societal Good, AI Innovation and Policy Coordination, Lead Scientific Advisor, Advisor for International Affairs.

## The AI Act Timeline

> **AI Omnibus 2026 postponement applied.** The high-risk effective dates below reflect the AI Omnibus postponement. See `../../../ai-act-high-risk/references/ai-omnibus-timeline-postponements.md` for the citation chain.

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1 August 2024 | Entry into force |
| 2 February 2025 | General provisions (definitions and AI literacy) and prohibitions apply |
| 2 August 2025 | Rules for general-purpose AI apply and governance must be in place |
| **2 December 2027** | Rules for high-risk AI (Annex III), transparency and measures in support of innovation apply *(Omnibus-postponed from 2 Aug 2026)* |
| **2 August 2028** | Rules for high-risk AI embedded in regulated products (Annex I) apply *(Omnibus-postponed from 2 Aug 2027)* |

## Implementation Priorities

### Set-up of Governance Structure

- Growing the AI Office.
- Collaboration with Member States in the AI Board.
- Establishing the Scientific Panel and Advisory Forum.

### Providing Guidance on Practical AI Act Application

- In-depth guidance documents and guidelines.
- Coordinating the development of stakeholder-driven instruments like standards and the code of practice on general-purpose AI.

### Stakeholder Outreach and Support in Compliance

- AI Pact network with more than 3,000 stakeholders, webinars and workshops.
- Upcoming AI Act Service Desk.

### Recent Activities

- Publication of a repository of good practices for AI literacy.
- Ongoing iterative drafting of Code of Practice on general-purpose AI.
- AI Pact webinars for an in-depth look into the AI Act.
- Publication of guidelines on the AI system definition and prohibitions.
