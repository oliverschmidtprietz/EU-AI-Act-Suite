## Purpose and Context

Under the EU AI Act, providers of high-risk AI systems will be required to report serious incidents to national authorities. This obligation, set out in **Article 73**, aims to:

- Detect risks early
- Ensure accountability
- Enable quick action
- Build public trust in AI technologies

The rules become applicable from **August 2026**. The draft guidance and reporting template are published in advance to help providers prepare.

Published 26 September 2025 (last updated 4 November 2025).

## Scope of the Draft Guidance

### Definitions and Practical Examples

The draft guidance clarifies definitions of serious incidents, offers practical examples, and explains how the new rules relate to other legal obligations.

### Alignment with International Initiatives

The EU's approach seeks alignment with international initiatives, such as the **OECD's AI Incidents Monitor** and **Common Reporting Framework**.
