## Overview

Opinion 28/2024 on certain data protection aspects related to the processing of personal data in the context of AI models, adopted on 18 December 2024.

This opinion was issued by the EDPB pursuant to Article 64(2) GDPR, following a request from the Irish Supervisory Authority (IE SA) concerning AI model processing. It represents the EDPB's authoritative guidance on how GDPR principles apply to the development and deployment of AI models, particularly large language models and other foundation models.

## Context

The opinion was prompted by:
- A formal Article 64(2) request from the Irish Supervisory Authority regarding AI model processing
- Growing regulatory attention to how personal data is used in training and operating AI models
- The need for consistent interpretation of GDPR requirements across EU member states in the AI context

The EDPB noted in its accompanying press release: "GDPR principles support responsible AI."

## Key Topics

### Legal Basis for Processing Personal Data in AI Models

The opinion addresses the legal bases available under Article 6 GDPR for the processing of personal data during the training and operation of AI models, including the application of legitimate interest assessments.

### Anonymity of AI Models

The opinion examines the conditions under which an AI model can be considered anonymous (i.e., not containing personal data), and the implications of this determination for GDPR compliance.

### Consequences of Unlawful Processing in Model Training

The opinion addresses the consequences where personal data has been unlawfully processed during the training phase of an AI model, including whether subsequent use of the model is necessarily unlawful.

### Data Protection by Design and by Default

The opinion provides guidance on how the principles of data protection by design and by default (Article 25 GDPR) apply to the development and deployment of AI models.

### Data Subject Rights

The opinion addresses the exercise of data subject rights (including access, rectification, erasure, and objection) in the context of AI models where personal data may be embedded in model weights.

### GDPR-AI Act Interplay

The opinion contributes to the broader regulatory framework by clarifying how GDPR obligations interact with the emerging requirements under the AI Act, particularly for AI model providers who are also subject to the AI Act's provisions on general-purpose AI models.
