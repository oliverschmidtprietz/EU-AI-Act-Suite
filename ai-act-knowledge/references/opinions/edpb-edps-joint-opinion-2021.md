## Overview

EDPB-EDPS Joint Opinion 5/2021 on the proposal for a Regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council laying down harmonised rules on artificial intelligence (Artificial Intelligence Act), adopted on 18 June 2021.

This Joint Opinion was issued during the legislative process for the AI Act, addressing the European Commission's original April 2021 proposal. It represents the data protection authorities' formal input on the regulation's design and its interplay with existing data protection law.

## Key Themes

Based on cross-references in subsequent EDPB/EDPS opinions (notably Joint Opinion 1/2026, Point 50), this opinion addressed:

### Scope and Definitions of the AI Act Proposal

The opinion examined the proposed regulation's scope, definitions, and classification of AI systems, including the risk-based approach.

### Data Protection and the AI Act

The opinion analysed the relationship between the proposed AI Act and existing EU data protection legislation, in particular the GDPR and the Law Enforcement Directive.

### Prohibited AI Practices

The EDPB and EDPS provided recommendations on the list of prohibited AI practices, including real-time remote biometric identification in publicly accessible spaces.

### High-Risk AI Systems

The opinion addressed the classification and requirements for high-risk AI systems, including requirements for training data, documentation, transparency, and human oversight.

### Grandfathering Clause

The EDPB and EDPS objected to the exemption from obligations for AI systems already placed on the market (the grandfathering clause), a position they reiterated in their 2026 Joint Opinion (Point 50).

### Role of Data Protection Authorities

The opinion advocated for a clear role for data protection authorities in the AI Act's governance framework, including in market surveillance and enforcement.

### Rights of Affected Persons

The EDPB and EDPS addressed the need for adequate safeguards and rights for individuals affected by AI systems.
