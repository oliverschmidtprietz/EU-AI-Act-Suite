## Project Overview

The Danish Institute for Human Rights is collaborating with the European Center for Not-For-Profit Law (ECNL) to develop guidance on fundamental rights impact assessments for high-risk AI systems.

**Project period:** January 2025 -- June 2025

**Contact:** Ioana Tuta, Senior Adviser, Human Rights, Tech and Business (iotu@humanrights.dk)

## Background

The EU Artificial Intelligence (AI) Act requires deployers of high-risk AI systems, prior to the deployment of the system, to perform an assessment of the impact on fundamental rights that the use of such systems may produce. In 2025, the European Commission is expected to publish a template to facilitate deployers, which can be both public and private entities, in complying with their obligation to conduct fundamental rights impact assessments (FRIA).

## Project Aim

The aim of this project is to develop guidance materials that can support the practical implementation of the FRIA provision in the AI Act and to ensure that the template under preparation by the European Commission is informed by international standards such as the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights as well as emerging good practice on human rights impact assessments.

The Institute and the European Center for Not-For-Profit Law will collaborate with a core group of experts across civil society, academia, and national human rights institutions.

The project is supported through a grant by the European Artificial Intelligence & Society Fund.

## Why This Matters

The AI Act entered into force in August 2024. It is one of the most comprehensive regulations on AI globally combining in an innovative manner considerations of product safety and fundamental rights protection. The AI Act attaches obligations to providers and deployers of high-risk AI systems to assess and mitigate their risks and impacts on fundamental rights.

Given that most negative human rights impacts of AI systems materialize after their deployment, the FRIA obligation has been recognized as a key human rights safeguard in the AI Act. However, the AI Act has under-developed provisions on important features of human rights impact assessments such as the paramount objective to prevent human rights abuses and the centrality of stakeholder engagement.

Through this project, the Institute and ECNL seek to ensure that such critical considerations are elevated and meaningfully factored in during the AI Act implementation.

## Key Publication

**A Guide to Fundamental Rights Impact Assessments under the EU Artificial Intelligence (AI) Act** -- This guide aims to support organisations that deploy high-risk AI systems to conduct fundamental rights impact assessments (FRIA) in accordance with the EU AI Act and international standards.
