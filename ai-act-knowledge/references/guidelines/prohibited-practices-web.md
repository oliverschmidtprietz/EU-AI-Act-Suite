## Purpose and Scope

The European Commission published the **Guidelines on prohibited artificial intelligence (AI) practices**, as defined by the AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689), on 4 February 2025. These guidelines provide an overview of AI practices that are deemed unacceptable due to their potential risks to European values and fundamental rights.

## Regulatory Context

The AI Act aims to promote innovation while ensuring high levels of health, safety, and fundamental rights protection. It classifies AI systems into different risk categories:

- **Prohibited** practices (Article 5)
- **High-risk** AI systems
- AI systems subject to **transparency obligations**

The guidelines specifically address prohibited practices, including:

- Harmful manipulation
- Social scoring
- Real-time remote biometric identification
- Other practices falling under Article 5(a)-(h)

## Legal Status of the Guidelines

The guidelines are designed to ensure the **consistent, effective, and uniform application** of the AI Act across the European Union. Key points regarding their legal status:

- They offer valuable insights into the Commission's interpretation of the prohibitions.
- They are **non-binding** -- authoritative interpretations are reserved for the Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU).
- They provide **legal explanations and practical examples** to help stakeholders understand and comply with the AI Act's requirements.

This initiative underscores the EU's commitment to fostering a safe and ethical AI landscape.
