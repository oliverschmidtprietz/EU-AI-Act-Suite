The AI Act Whistleblower Tool empowers individuals to securely submit a report and contribute directly to making AI in Europe safe, transparent, and trustworthy.

## Purpose and Role of Whistleblowers

Whistleblowers play a vital role in identifying potential violations of the law that could endanger fundamental rights, health, or public trust, and which might otherwise go undetected. By reporting potential violations, whistleblowers can support the AI Office in detecting them early on, thereby contributing to the safe and transparent development of AI technologies.

## The Whistleblower Tool

The [AI Act Whistleblower Tool](https://ai-act-whistleblower.integrityline.app/), launched by the AI Office, allows individuals who are professionally connected to an AI model provider to report harmful practices by providers of general-purpose AI models and certain AI systems.

### Submission Features

- Reports can be submitted **anonymously** in any EU language
- Supporting documents can be attached
- A **secure inbox** enables whistleblowers to:
  - Stay up to date on the progress of their report
  - Answer follow-up questions while remaining anonymous

## Protections and Confidentiality

### Current Protections (Until 2 August 2026)

The AI Office commits itself to a high standard of confidentiality and has documented internal procedures to maximise the protection of whistleblowers' identities. While these robust safeguards ensure confidentiality, it is important to note that **legal protection against retaliation under the Whistleblower Directive will only extend to reports concerning infringements of the AI Act from 2 August 2026 onwards**. Until then, confidentiality remains the primary means of protection for whistleblowers.

### Existing Legal Protections

Some AI-related activities may already fall within the scope of the [Whistleblower Directive (Directive (EU) 2019/1937)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2019/1937/oj/eng) and thus benefit from its protection, particularly those linked to:

- Product safety
- Consumer protection
- Privacy and personal data
- Information security

### Full Protection (From 2 August 2026)

From 2 August 2026, the Whistleblower Directive's full retaliation protections will apply to reports of AI Act infringements.

## Scope of Reports

Reports should concern harmful practices by:

- Providers of general-purpose AI models
- Providers of certain AI systems

The reporter should be **professionally connected** to the AI model provider in question.
