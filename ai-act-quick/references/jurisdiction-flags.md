# Jurisdiction Flags for Quick AI Act Triage

Condensed jurisdiction-specific requirements for rapid triage. Provides the dual-compliance-layer diagram and key country flags for quick assessment.

**Last verified:** March 2026. Always search for latest national implementation status.

---

## Dual Compliance Layer

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ WORKPLACE AI DEPLOYMENT — DUAL COMPLIANCE LAYER                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                 │
│  Layer 1: EU AI Act                                            │
│  ├── Art. 26(7): Inform employees about AI use                 │
│  ├── Annex III Nr. 4: Employment AI → high-risk                │
│  ├── Art. 5(1)(f): Emotion recognition → prohibited            │
│  └── Art. 27: FRIA for certain deployers                       │
│                                                                 │
│  Layer 2: National Employment Law                              │
│  ├── DE: Betriebsvereinbarung required (§87 BetrVG)            │
│  ├── AT: Dignity-based consent (§96 ArbVG)                     │
│  ├── CH: Personality protection (OR Art. 328)                  │
│  ├── FR: CSE consultation (L. 2312-38)                         │
│  ├── NL: Works council consent (Art. 27 WOR)                   │
│  ├── IT: Trade union agreement or inspectorate auth (Art. 4)   │
│  └── ES: Algorithm disclosure to worker reps (RD-ley 9/2021)   │
│                                                                 │
│  ⚠ National law often requires action BEFORE deployment         │
│  ⚠ AI Act obligations are IN ADDITION to national law           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Country Quick-Reference Checklists

### Germany
- [ ] Works council consulted (§87(1) Nr. 6 BetrVG) — before deployment if workplace AI
- [ ] Betriebsvereinbarung negotiated (if works council exists)
- [ ] BaFin requirements addressed (if financial sector)
- [ ] BSI/KRITIS obligations checked (if critical infrastructure)
- [ ] DPIA completed with BfDI/state DPA requirements

### Austria
- [ ] Works council consulted (§96(1) Nr. 3 ArbVG) — consent if dignity-affecting
- [ ] FMA requirements addressed (if financial sector)
- [ ] DPIA completed with DSB requirements

### Switzerland
- [ ] AI Act relevance assessed — does output reach EU market? (Art. 2(1)(c))
- [ ] nDSG Art. 21 automated decision rights addressed
- [ ] FINMA requirements addressed (if financial sector)
- [ ] No works council co-determination — but information rights apply

### France
- [ ] CSE consultation completed (L. 2312-38) — before AI introduction
- [ ] CNIL AI Compliance Guide reviewed
- [ ] ACPR/AMF requirements addressed (if financial sector)
- [ ] Platform worker obligations met (if gig economy)

### Netherlands
- [ ] Works council consent obtained (Art. 27(1)(l) WOR) — if monitoring tech
- [ ] AP algorithmic framework applied (Toetsingskader)
- [ ] DNB/AFM requirements addressed (if financial sector)

### Italy
- [ ] Art. 4 Statuto dei Lavoratori: trade union agreement or inspectorate auth (if monitoring AI)
- [ ] Decreto Trasparenza employee information provided
- [ ] Banca d'Italia/CONSOB/IVASS requirements addressed (if financial sector)

### Spain
- [ ] AESIA registration/notification checked
- [ ] ET Art. 64(4)(d) algorithm info to works council
- [ ] RD-ley 9/2021 transparency met (if platform workers)
- [ ] BdE/CNMV requirements addressed (if financial sector)
